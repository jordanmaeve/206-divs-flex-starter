The epipelagic zone, also known as the "sunlight zone" of the ocean, spans from the ocean's surface to 200m below it. At this stage, sunlight can still penetrate the water, and thus most creatures and photosynthesizing aquatic plant life lives here.

The mesopelagic zone of the ocean spans from 200-1000m below the ocean's surface. Here, sunlight is scarce, and many creatures that live here have adapted to the darkness with special features such as low light vision and bioluminescence.

The bathypelagic zone of the ocean spans from 1000-4000m below the ocean's surface. Sunlight doesn't penetrate here at all, and thus many creatures here live in total darkness. Some of them have even adapted to this environment in more radical ways, such as not having any eyes.
